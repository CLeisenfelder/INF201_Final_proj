An Overview

Olympic Weightlifting is a sport consisting of three exercises: the snatch, and the clean and jerk. In competition, the weight lifted between each exercise is added together and the lifter with the highest total wins.

There are many people to perform the olympic lifts as lesuirely exercise rather than for competition, and this can provide great benefits for the body. Further discussed in The Lifts section of this site, when performed under proper instruction the olympic lifts are a great way to strengthen joints at their end-ranges of motion. This is often overlooked in traditional strength training (some may argue that olympic lifting IS traditional strength training), and can lead to injury.
